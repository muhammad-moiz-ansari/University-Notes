So the history of computing starts with chips containing few transistors. However, with the passage of time, transistors became smaller and smaller due to advancement in technology. Now there are millions of transistors in each chip improving clock and frequency. It was predicted by the CEO of intel, Moore's Law, which showed a linear increment in the transistors per chip timeline graph. But at certain time, it was realized that transistors cannot become more small due to two reasons: Heat dissipation and engineering technology for making it more small. Now we got enough high computing power but the software development in order to achieve full advantage of high computing is still behind. Due to which currently High performance Computing is a trending research field. Further, we have more cores in a single processor so in order to divide an applications task into different core has become a challenging task.
